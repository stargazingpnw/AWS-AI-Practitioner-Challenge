# AWS AI Practitioner Challenge: PartyRock Portfolio
### Exploring the Gut-Brain Connection Through AI-Powered Product Design and Data Analysis

Welcome to my project portfolio for the **AWS AI Practitioner Challenge** (Udacity). This repository showcases two projects built using **Amazon PartyRock**: an AI-powered wellness coaching app, and an applied data analysis investigation using a real clinical dataset.

### 💡 The Thread Connecting Both Projects

This portfolio was inspired by *The Psychobiotic Revolution: Mood, Food, and the New Science of the Gut-Brain Connection*, which sparked a deeper interest in how diet and gut health influence mood and wellbeing. That curiosity shaped both projects here: one approaches the mood-food connection as a **builder**, designing a tool people can use day to day; the other approaches it as an **analyst**, interrogating real metabolomic data to understand what that relationship actually looks like.

Together, they reflect a genuine interest in healthtech, particularly in technical program and project management roles where understanding the underlying science is essential to building credible, effective products.

## 📂 Portfolio Projects

### 🏃 [Project 1: NutriWellness Personal Health Coach](./README_Project1.md)

**Core Focus:** Morning wellness coaching through nutrition, fitness, and mood support

A single-session AI app that acts as a daily morning coach. Users log yesterday's food intake and steps, select their current mood, and receive focused, executable guidance for today, calibrated against their body metrics, dietary restrictions, and food allergies.

**Highlights:** Designed strict prompt guardrails enforcing six dietary categories and food allergy compliance after catching the AI suggesting non-compliant foods during testing. Iteratively simplified every output widget down to two to three actionable items to reduce cognitive overwhelm, rather than overwhelming the user with comprehensive but unusable information.

**[View project folder →](https://github.com/stargazingpnw/AWS-AI-Practitioner-Challenge/tree/main/project-1-nutriwellness)**

### 🧪 [Project 2: Gut Microbiome and Metabolome Data Analysis](./README_Project2.md)

**Core Focus:** Applied data analysis using AI to explore a real clinical dataset

Used PartyRock's Analyze Data feature (Whiskers) to investigate a peer-reviewed dataset examining gut microbiome and metabolome profiles in food-secure versus food-insecure college students. Asked targeted analytical questions, generated a combined analysis table, and critically evaluated the accuracy and usefulness of the AI's findings.

**Highlights:** Identified and explained several counterintuitive findings, including an inverse relationship between BMI and branched-chain amino acids that runs against typical obesity research, demonstrating evaluation of AI output rather than uncritical acceptance.

**[View project folder →](https://github.com/stargazingpnw/AWS-AI-Practitioner-Challenge/tree/main/project-2-data-analysis)**

## 🧠 Core Competencies Demonstrated

- **Prompt engineering and constraint design:** Writing precise instructions to control AI output, enforce safety-critical formatting (dietary and allergy compliance), and reduce inconsistency across outputs
- **Working within platform constraints:** Navigating PartyRock's no-code limitations (no auth, no data persistence, single-session state) and redesigning scope to still deliver a genuinely useful tool
- **Critical evaluation of AI output:** Identifying when AI-generated insights made sense, when they needed correction (incorrect food suggestions), and when surprising results warranted deeper interpretation rather than dismissal
- **Responsible AI use:** Prioritizing user safety in health-adjacent recommendations, avoiding collection of unnecessary personal data, and building in explicit guardrails for dietary and allergy compliance
